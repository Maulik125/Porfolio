A.P.J Abdul kalam

1931 - 2015

Missle Man of India

Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931-2015) was an eminent Indian scientist and a highly respected political figure who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He is often referred to as "Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam" or simply "Dr. Kalam." Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, on October 15, 1931, Kalam came from a modest background. He had an early interest in science and was particularly fascinated by flight. He pursued aeronautical engineering and graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1954. He later joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he made significant contributions to India's missile and space programs.
